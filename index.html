Recipe #1: Cookies

  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Total Number of Servings: 4 servings, 48 cookies
Ingredients Quantity
Granulated Sugar 3/4 cup
Packed Brown Sugar 3/4 cup
Butter or Margarine, softened 1 cup
Vanilla 1 teaspoon
Egg 1
Gold Medal™ All-Purpose Flour 2 1/4 cups
Baking soda 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Coarsely chopped nuts 1 cup
Semisweet chocolate chips 1/2 cup

Step 1: Heat oven to 375ºF.
Step 2: Mix sugars, butter, vanilla and egg in large bowl. Stir in flour, baking soda and salt (dough will be stiff). Stir in nuts and chocolate chips.
Step 3: Drop dough by rounded tablespoonfuls about 2 inches apart onto ungreased cookie sheet.
Step 4: Bake 8 to 10 minutes or until light brown (centers will be soft). Cool slightly; remove from cookie sheet. Cool on wire rack.
Step 5: Eat!

Source: Betty Crocker

Recipe #2: Chocolate Doughnut Cupcakes

Want to visit the top of the page?

Let's advance in the baking chapter, shall we?

  • Time: 1 hour and 45 minutes
  • Total Number of Servings: 18
Ingredients Quantity
Devil's Food
Chocolate Cake Doughnuts 		12
Whipping Cream 1 cup
Vanilla 1 teaspoon
Eggs 3
Semisweet Chocolate Chips 3/4 cup
Betty Crocker™ Rich &
Creamy Chocolate Frosting 		1 container (1 lb)
Multi Colored Candy Sprinkles 1/2 cup

Step 1: Heat oven to 400°F. Place paper baking cup in each of 18 regular-size muffin cups..
Step 2:Chop doughnuts. In large bowl, mix whipping cream, vanilla and eggs with wire whisk until blended. Gently fold in chopped doughnuts and chocolate chips. Divide batter evenly among muffin cups, filling each with about 1/3 cup batter or until almost full.
Step 3: Bake 17 to 19 minutes or until set. Cool 10 minutes; remove cupcakes from pans to cooling rack. Cool completely.
Step 4:Spoon frosting into decorating bag fitted with large round tip. Pipe frosting around edge of each cupcake, leaving center unfrosted. Decorate with sprinkles. Store loosely covered.
Step 5: Eat!

Source: Betty Crocker

Recipe #3: Tiramisu Cake

Want to visit the top of the page?

We've finally reached the final stage: The Hardest Recipe!

  • Time: 5 hours and 0 minutes
  • Servings: 12 pieces
Ingredients Quantity
Betty Crocker™ Super Moist™
Yellow Cake Mix (add water vegetable oil, and eggs to cake mix as instructed on box)(for cake) 		1 box
Hot Brewed Espresso Coffee or very strong coffee (for coffee syrup) 3/4 cup
Cognac (for coffee syrup) 2 tablespoons
Powdered Sugar (for coffee syrup) 2 tablespoons
Mascarpone Cheese (for filling) 3 cups
Powdered Sugar (for filling) 1 1/2 cups
Cognac (for filling) 2 tablespoons
Vanilla (for filling) 3 teaspoons
Cold Whipping Cream (for filling) 1 cup
Dutch processed or regular unsweetened baking
cocoa (for garnish) 		3 tablespoons
Chocolate covered coffee beans (for garnish) 1 handful
Dark baking chocolate, coarsely chopped (for garnish) 3 oz

Step 1 :Heat oven to 350°F (325°F for dark or nonstick pans). Grease 2 (9-inch) round cake pans with shortening; line pans with cooking parchment paper. Make cake mix as directed on box, using water, oil and eggs. Pour batter into pans.
Step 2:Bake 25 to 29 minutes or until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean. Cool 10 minutes; remove from pans to cooling racks. Cool completely.
Step 3:In small bowl, mix coffee syrup ingredients. Set aside to cool.
Step 4: Meanwhile, in large bowl, beat mascarpone cheese, 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar, 2 tablespoons cognac and the vanilla with electric mixer on medium speed until smooth. In small bowl, beat whipping cream with electric mixer on high speed until stiff peaks form. Gently fold whipped cream into mascarpone mixture until combined.
Step 5: To assemble, cut each cake horizontally to make 2 layers. Place 1 cake layer on plate; moisten generously with coffee syrup, then spread with about 1 cup of the filling. Repeat with remaining 3 cake layers. Spread remaining filling over top and side of cake.
Step 6:Sprinkle cocoa over cake. Arrange coffee beans around top edge of cake. Sprinkle chopped chocolate around bottom edge of cake. Refrigerate at least 3 hours before serving.
Step 7: EAT!
